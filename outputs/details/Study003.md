# Study 003 – Eye Mind Wander Database: Complete Dataset Details

## Source
Wong, A. Y., Bryck, R. L., Baker, R. S., Hutt, S., & Mills, C. (2023, March). "Using a webcam based eye-tracker to understand students' thought patterns" in LAK23: 13th International Learning Analytics and Knowledge Conference (pp. 453-463). https://doi.org/10.1145/3576050.3576115

## Methodology Details

**Eye Tracking Technology:** Webcam-based system (WebGazer)

**Participant Population:** Undergraduate students

**Primary Task:** Sequential paragraph reading (one paragraph presented at a time)

**Task Classification:** Reading comprehension activity

**Performance Outcomes:** Not applicable for this study

## Measurement Framework

**Mind Wandering Indicators:**
- Thought-Unrelated-to-Task (TUT)
- Awareness levels
- Fluctuation Measure of Task-engagement (FMT)
- Disengagement patterns

**Gaze-Related Metrics:**
- Total gazes
- Unique gazes
- Offscreen gaze occurrences
- Area-of-Interest (AOI) gazes

**Temporal Window:** Analysis based on 2 paragraphs preceding each probe administration

**Area of Interest:** The text paragraph itself

## Analytical Notes
AOI gaze proportions and offscreen gaze proportions utilize modified calculation methods to ensure consistency across the database, differing from the original paper's computational approach.
