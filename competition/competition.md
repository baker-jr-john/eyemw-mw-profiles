# Eye Mind Wander Dataset Competition

Source: [https://www.eyemindwander.com/competition/](https://www.eyemindwander.com/competition/)

## Overview

The Affect, Cognition, and Computation Lab at the University of Minnesota and Penn Center for Learning Analytics at the University of Pennsylvania jointly present the Eye, Mind, Wander Data Competition. This initiative offers participants anonymized observational data from learners' eye gaze and behavioral measures. The dataset includes "in-situ thought probes" capturing task-unrelated thoughts, freely-moving thought, valence, and arousal during learning activities like reading, video, and VR experiences across diverse learner populations.

Competitors are encouraged to "produce novel scientific insights into attention, cognition, engagement, or other scientific areas" with potential applications to learning science, cognitive science, and learning analytics.

## Competition Data

- Eye measures and probe response data from the Eye Mind Wander collection
- Participants may use any datasets from the collection or combine multiple datasets
- Supplementary metadata available, including reading proficiency measures, text difficulty levels, and demographic information
- All data is fully de-identified and IRB-compliant

## Accepted Methods

- Statistics
- Analytics, data mining, and machine learning
- Quantitative ethnography
- Analysis using pre-trained models and large language models

## Suggested Research Directions

- Predicting mind wandering from eye-tracking data
- Investigating reading comprehension strategies and inattention relationships
- Creating real-time attention and boredom detection metrics
- Exploring mind wandering antecedents and differentiating factors

## Submission Requirements

- Short paper with research question, methods, results, and implications
- No strict formatting requirements
- Recommended supplementary code in public repositories
- Submission link to be announced

## Evaluation Criteria

- Technical rigor and reproducibility
- Scientific importance and novelty
- Clarity for interdisciplinary audiences
